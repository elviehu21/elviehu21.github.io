Timeline for the development of menstrual products:

1911: Midol made the first pain killer for general pain. Cellucotton was used as a bandage in WWI.

1919: Woll worth in Chicago sold their first Kotex made of cellucotton, however, women back in those days relied on homemade clothing pads.

1920: Women used a pad belt and overnight bloomers from Sears catalog. The ad for these pad belts were that periods were embarrassing for females.

1930: Doctors treated periods like a disability and refrained females from doing physical activities. The tampon inside a tube was created and Gertrude Tenderich founded Tampax.

1940: Health sponges were used as a washable sponge that soaks up menstraul blood.

1950: Sanitary belts and tampons were popular. 25 million Kotex boxes were sold every month.

1970: Stayfree Mini-Pads and Kotex added an adhesive strip at the bottom- creation of the moden-day pad. Super-absorbent tampons have become more popualar.

1980: Toxic Shock Syndrome put tampons out of regular usage. Pads were thriving.

1990: Maxi-Pads with wins became popular. It was considered that if you used a tampon, teen thought they would not be virgins anymore.

2000: Birth Control.

2010: 80% of people will either use tampons or maxi-pads. However, the menstrual cup is alive and thriving for the betterment of the person and the environment.
